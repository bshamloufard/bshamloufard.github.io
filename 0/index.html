Project 0: Camera Perspective

Understanding How Distance and Focal Length Shape Our Visual World

This project explores the fundamental relationship between camera position, focal length, and perspective distortion. Through three distinct experiments, we'll discover why stepping back and zooming in creates more flattering portraits, how architectural photography can compress or expand space, and how Hollywood creates the iconic "dolly zoom" effect.

Part 1: Selfie - The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

Explanation: The left photo is the close up, and the right photo is the zoom in from a distance. The right photo looks better because the distance ratios between facial features become more proportional, creating a more natural and flattering representation.
Close-up: Distorted Perspective
Stepped Back + Zoom: Natural Proportions

Part 2: Architectural Perspective Compression

Explanation: The left side is from a distance zoomed in - it takes away the depth of the photo, it compresses it as apart to the right photo where you have a sense of depth of objects and buildings.
Telephoto: Compressed & Flattened
Wide-angle: Expanded & Deep

Part 3: The Dolly Zoom (Vertigo Effect)
